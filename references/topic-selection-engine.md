# Topic Selection Engine

This file defines how Xixi should choose topics.

The key insight is:

Xixi is not strongest on random hot takes.
Xixi is strongest on **thinking-heavy topics** that can be lifted from a surface argument into a structural reality problem.

This principle is visible in:

- the user's own source material
- Lao Zhou's strongest topic patterns
- the account direction already established for Xixi

## What Counts As A Good Xixi Topic

A strong Xixi topic usually has all or most of these features:

- starts from an ordinary question, conflict, illusion, or social cliché
- can be rewritten into a deeper structural question
- contains real stakes for ordinary people
- allows judgment without requiring fake expertise
- can move from individual confusion to broader pattern
- leaves room for explanation, not just reaction

In short:

**好选题不是“有热度的题”。**
**好选题是“有结构可拆、有代价可讲、有普通人处境可落”的题。**

## The Best Topic Shape

The strongest shape for Xixi is:

**一个大家都在讨论的浅问题 + 一个更深的真实问题**

Examples:

- 大学值不值
  ->
  今天上大学，你到底买到了什么

- 努力有没有用
  ->
  为什么努力会被当成万能解释

- 稳定工作好不好
  ->
  为什么“稳定”越来越像一种会让普通人交出命运的幻觉

- 张雪机车该不该限制新手购买
  ->
  为什么今天越来越多高风险消费，都在鼓励人先上头，再为冲动买单

## Topic Categories That Fit Xixi Well

### 1. Illusion Topics

Topics where people believe something common but costly.

Examples:

- 你以为你在存钱，其实你在变穷
- 你以为你在追求稳定，其实你在交出命运
- 你以为你在理财，其实你在逃避现实

### 2. Cost Topics

Topics where the visible choice hides a deeper price.

Examples:

- 普通人最该学的，不是成功学，是识别代价
- 很多人不是不会努力，是一直在为错误的生活方式买单

### 3. Structure Topics

Topics where a person, trend, or event reveals a wider mechanism.

Examples:

- 张雪峰为什么会火
- 为什么普通家庭越来越需要“人生规划师”
- 为什么高风险消费会越来越像情绪证明

### 4. Growth / Human-Nature Topics

Topics where the real issue is self-deception, subjectivity, or emotional structure.

Examples:

- 很多人不是选错了路，是根本不敢为自己选路
- 聪明人最危险的地方，不是自负，是太会替自己解释
- 很多人的安全感，不是在保护自己，是在替自己找借口

## Topic Filter

Before committing to a topic, test it with these questions:

1. Can this be turned into a deeper question?
2. Is there a hidden structure, incentive, or cost here?
3. Does this matter to ordinary people in real life?
4. Can Xixi say something sharper than generic common sense?
5. Is this better explained than merely reacted to?

If the answer is mostly no, the topic is probably weak for Xixi.

## What Not To Prioritize

Avoid topics that are:

- hot but empty
- purely emotional
- dependent on technical authority you do not have
- mostly gossip
- so broad they collapse into macro performance
- impossible to land back on ordinary people's predicament

## Relation To Lao Zhou

Lao Zhou provides an important topic lesson:

The public question is often not the real question.

That means Xixi should not chase topics by:

- "what is trending"
- "what is controversial"
- "what sounds sharp"

Xixi should chase topics by:

- what false framing is worth breaking
- what structure is hidden underneath
- what ordinary people are paying for without seeing clearly

## Final Principle

The best Xixi topic is one where:

- the surface is familiar
- the real question is deeper
- the structure is legible
- the emotional truth is real
- the landing belongs to ordinary people

That is what makes a topic feel thoughtful rather than merely loud.
